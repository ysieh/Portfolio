What's That Color? | Neural Net Research


Supervisors: David Plaut // Duration: March 2017 - May 2017
Contribution: Research, development, and analysis of neural networks; academic writing

"How would a neural network decide to name a color when provided with its RGB code? What does 'blue' mean in through the metaphorical eyes of our computers?"

During my sophomore fall semester, I worked in Folia Water's science department. I focused on microbiological testing of the Folia Filters product, a silver-based filter paper that serves as a cheap water filtration solution for those in need.


Link to paper

Background Research

In this project, I developed a series of neural networks that sought to label colors based on their RGB values. Each network was trained on a variety of different inputs (families of colors, or groups of colors to simulate the color wheel) and were then tested against groups of target colors and evaluated for overall correctness, accuracy per trial, and for speed. Results were gathered for each network and presented in the form of a research paper.
